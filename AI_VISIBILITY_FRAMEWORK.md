# AI Visibility Framework

A six-stage methodology developed by [Jonomor](https://www.jonomor.com) that defines how organizations achieve AI Visibility — the ability to be recognized, retrieved, and cited by AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Copilot.

## The Six Stages

### Stage 1: Entity Stability
Establishing your organization as a disambiguated entity in knowledge systems. Your name, type, description, and identifiers must be consistent and machine-readable across every surface where you appear.

### Stage 2: Category Ownership
Claiming your position within the knowledge graph for your category. AI systems must associate your entity with the topics, industries, and domains where you operate.

### Stage 3: Schema Graph
Providing structured, machine-readable data via JSON-LD schema that AI systems can parse directly. This includes entity declarations, relationship graphs, and offer structures.

### Stage 4: Reference Surfaces
Building external authority through third-party references, mentions, backlinks, and citations that validate your entity's claims and establish credibility beyond your own domain.

### Stage 5: Knowledge Index
Ensuring your entity and its associated content are retrievable by AI systems during their knowledge-building and response-generation processes.

### Stage 6: Continuous Signal Surfaces
The operational layer — consistent content production, structured outreach, mention tracking, and AI citation monitoring. The first five stages are architectural. Stage 6 is operational. It compounds over time.

## Scoring

The AI Visibility Framework uses a 50-point scoring methodology across all six stages.

- **Authority** (40+): The entity is consistently cited by AI answer engines
- **Viable** (25–39): The entity has structural AI Visibility but gaps remain
- **Fragmented** (below 25): The entity lacks the architecture for AI citation
